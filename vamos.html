Vamos

A vocabulary responsive app

Overview

Vamos is a learning vocabulary application for users to improve their language skills and grammar that can be easily used around a chaotic schedule.

My Role

  • Research & Competitive Analysis
  • Wireframes
  • UI Design
  • Prototyping
  • Usability Testing

Tools I used

  • Pen & Paper
  • Google Forms
  • Google Forms
  • Slack
  • Figma

Time

July 2021 - August 2021

Problem Statement

Vamos users need a responsive web application that they employ to study on a phone, tablet, or computer, because they want to be able to resume their learning whenever they have free time no matter their location.

Hypothesis

We believe that by creating an app that offers articles and podcasts, besides lessons, we will achieve the goal of helping users stay motivated and constant with their lessons.

Research

Competitive Analysis

I did a Competitive Analysis of the two vocabulary application that helps the user to improve their language of choice, Quizlet and Atlas English to learn what they were offering and get insights into their strengths and weakness.

Quizlet

An app that helps users study and master new words in any language of their choice.

Strengths

  • Quizlet has a very encouraging sign-up front-page screen, simple layout, and many choices to create your own study guide.
  • On settings the user can choose from a wide range of international languages either for the term or definition.
  • On settings the user can choose from a wide range of international languages either for the term or definition.

Weakness

  • The screen is very dark and I do not think this works for a majority of users, It would be nice to have the option to switch it.
  • The size of the letters is very small, making the user’s eyes get tired after a while.
  • They are ads popping up all the time on the screen and whenever you finish a set you will get a 5-10 second commercials video.

Atlas English

An app that improves your English with a wide selection of categories like words, idioms, and phrases.

Strengths

  • Users can promptly set up reminders when starting to use the app and there is no need to sign up before exploring and taking a look.
  • Users can read articles, find definitions and examples in the same place.
  • The app will let you know when you get the wrong one, showing you the right one and moving on to the next question.

Weakness

  • The atlas app has a lot to explore and there is not any type of tutorial or guide of how to use it.
  • The app does not let the users how many they got right or wrong, making it hard to track your performance.
  • The dark background and letter size could be problematic for some users.

User Interviews

I conducted interviews with regular users of vocabulary or language apps. The goal is to better understand how people approach learning a new vocabulary or language. ​​​​​​​I was able to better understand potential users' experiences and what they were looking for.

Interview Insights

  • Real-time lessons with supervision that you can ask for guidance or questions and a way to connect to other students/users to practice.
  • To work towards a diploma as a goal.
  • Some features in apps I will enjoy would be constant reminders and updates on the progress of my classes.
  • Even though an app might not be the best to learn 100% I think instant gratification and gaming are great methods to get you motivated.
  • An option where you can practice your vocabulary and conversation skills with real people like a video chat platform where you could connect with other users will be great!

Ideation

User Personas

I created personas, Ben and Kirsten with needs and goals to help me to visualize real users and design a product that meets their needs.

User Flow

Focusing on Ben, I built a task analysis and user flow. With this user flow, I was able to visualize what the user will need to complete the journey of listening to a podcast.

Wireframes

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Now I was ready to start sketching the design ideas I have for the app. I sketched the onboarding, finding a podcast, and learning with flashcards.

Usability Testing

The goal was to test the low-fidelity prototype Vamos with real people before designing the mid-fidelity wireframes and get insights into what is working so far.

Observations

  • Participants were unclear about how many times they get to enter an answer before moving to the next flashcard.
  • The podcast’s feature category is unclear on the navigation bar.
  • The podcast feature should have grammar/subtitles since this is a learning vocabulary app..

Quotes

  • “I really like the app, the animation and setup is pretty cool.”
  • “I love the idea of having a podcast feature when learning vocabulary.”
  • “I like that the flashcard section asks if you want to repeat the session”

Conclusion

When testing the prototype I learn most of the participants like guidance and be engaged with helpful messages of tips and heads up but at the same time short and straightforward. Also having animation encourages and makes them feel at ease.

Conclusion

Learnings from this design are:

  • Users like guidance and engaging messages when learning.
  • Animated apps are more enjoyable and incentive.
  • User-friendly features that are short and straightforward are preferred.
  • New ideas might emerge during the interaction and testing steps.
  • Hand-drawing prototypes can be time-consuming. People's opinions can take you off your path.
  • Some features you invented might be unnecessary or impractical.

Next Steps

  • Create and develop more features like a chat room, help center, library, etc...
  • Design the digital wireframes and prototype. Run more usability tests.
  • Polish the app design.
