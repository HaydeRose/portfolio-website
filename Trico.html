Trico

The way you pay, send, and get money

Overview

Trico is an e-wallet application prototype designed to offer an intuitive, and secure e-wallet for users to transfer money, shop online, and pay in-store. This app is designed for users that enjoy and feel comfortable around technology and want to manage all their transactions in one place for peace of mind and contribute to a reduction in climate change with wireless transactions and using less or no paper at all.

Problem Statement

Trico users need a way to securely and smoothly transfer money, shop online, and pay in-store because they want to be able to have an app that manages all of their transactions in one place.

Hypothesis

We believe that by designing an intuitive and user-friendly responsive payment application, where users can have access to their account information, and perform money transfers, as in-store and online payments, we will achieve the goal of helping them manage and accomplish all financial transactions in one place.

Research

Competitive Analysis

To understand the market and users, I researched what the market was offering and got insights into the problems they were facing. I did a Competitive Analysis of the two biggest payments application in the market Venmo and Google Pay to learn what they were offering and get insights into their strengths and weakness.

Venmo Analysis

Venmo is a social payments service to make and share payments with friends, family, and select approved businesses.

Strengths

  • Users can make wireless payments at participants' businesses.
  • Works on iOS, Android, and the Web.
  • It is listed as a payment option on many websites and apps that accept PayPal.

Weakness

  • No international payments outside the USA.
  • Scammers are known to take advantage of app users.
  • Privacy concerns by default settings.

Google Analysis

Google Pay is a digital wallet platform and online payment system developed by Google to power in-app, online, and in-person contactless purchases on mobile devices, enabling users to make payments with Androids phones, tablets, or watches.

Strengths

  • Users can easily pay in-store using the NFC method.
  • Substitute users’ card numbers with virtual ones for security.
  • Offers and supports rewards and loyalty programs options.

Weakness

  • Does Not work with Google or Siri voice assistant.
  • No Buyers protections.
  • Doesn't let you pay friends with a credit card as some competitors do.

User Interviews

I conducted three interviews with colleagues that use payments apps on a regular basis. ​​​​​​​I was able to better understand what potential users' experiences, needs, and goals were, as well as identify habits when employing a payment app.

Interview Insights

  • All of the participants find apps to be essential in their life and are using them to pay rent, do online shopping, in-store and transfer money between peers, and even last-minute gifts!
  • Participant’s most-used apps are Apple Pay, Venmo, and Google Pay.
  • Participants find PayPal login and navigation through the app tedious and confusing, one said: “It is like an endless portal”.
  • Security Protocols are very important for users when using an app and most of the participants don't really understand how their apps are implementing them.
  • Participants will use their apps 80% on a phone vs 20% on a computer.
  • 80% of the participants will choose to use their payment app vs a physical card.

Ideation

User Personas

I wanted to visualize the goals and needs of potential users when employing a payment application. With these personas, I can better design a product to fulfill their needs and create user-friendly experiences.

User Flow

Focusing on persona 2, I built a task analysis and user flow. With this user flow, I was able to visualize what the user will need to complete the journey of sending money.

Wireframes

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

Now I was ready to start sketching the design ideas I have for the app. The three core features of the application: Reminders, Sending Money, and Online Payments Set Up.

Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

When designing the Mid-Fidelity Wireframes, I named the Send & Request buttons instead of just using icons, felt more user-friendly and less confusing.

Usability Testing

I recruited six users to conduct a Usability Test to help me evaluate the prototype and improve the design of the app. The goal was to observe the users to perform the tasks with the main features and identify any friction or pain points.

Positive Feedbacks

  • The overall navigation of the app is pretty smooth.
  • Setting up the online payment was clear.
  • The participants like the Reminders feature.

Observations

  • A confirmation when finishing setting up online payment will be useful.
  • Two participants said, "I would like confirmation before sending the money".
  • Two participants skipped the onboarding and went straight to create an account.

Pain Points

  • Three participants could not find the reminders feature.
  • Five participants looked for the reminders button at the top of the screen.
  • All participants tried to log out from the profile picture.

Conclusion

Most of the participants quickly found their way through the app and perform the tasks asked. Still, there were a couple of things like making the profile picture a shortcut for personal info and log out, merging accounts and tools, and moving the new reminder button will help make the app more user-friendly.

Hight-Fidelity Wireframes

After resolving the issues in the Mid-Fidelity Wireframes I went ahead and designed the High-Fidelity wireframes. During the process of refining and polishing these wireframes many things changed but the core idea of the app stayed the same.

Design System

A Design Language System was created to document the rules and guidelines for the Trico Application. For Trico's system, I wanted strong and bold colors, typography, and images that will make the user feel calm and empowered by their finances.

Prototype

The wireframes UI were further refined and improved by following the Gestalt Principles and Accessibility Design.

Conclusion

Now you are wondering if I resolve the problem statement and fulfill the needs and goals of the three personas representing users?

To resolve this problem I designed the following features:

  • Money Transfers: Victoria and Santiago will be able to send or request money to contacts individually or by the group.
  • Language Setting: There is a language setting option on the account category for Victoria to choose her language of choice.
  • Reminders: for those with a busy schedule and need a little reminder when needed as Victoria with her monthly rent payments.
  • Paying Online: Josh will be able to add any card or bank of his choice under the payments methods to pay online when checking out.
  • Rewards: with this program, Josh will be able to collect points whenever using the app and redeem them later for cash to be used in the app.
